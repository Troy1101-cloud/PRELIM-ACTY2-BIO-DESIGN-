“Through Every Season: The Life of Troy Winter”

Childhood Years

1 I’m Troy Winter T. Andrada, born on April 1, 2007 in Antipolo, Rizal. 2 For my first four years, we lived there before moving to Cainta, Rizal. 3 I am the youngest among six siblings—five boys and one girl. 4 Even as a child, I was naturally curious about my surroundings. 5 I remember my first day at Summerville when I was four years old. 6 I felt nervous but excited to meet my classmates. 7 My parents always encouraged me to do my best. 8 My siblings and I played outside every afternoon. 9 I loved riding my bike around the neighborhood. 10 We enjoyed games like hide-and-seek and tag. 11 I often helped my mom in the kitchen. 12 Holidays and birthdays were special because our family was complete. 13 I won a small award during my first-grade years. 14 My teachers described me as curious and eager to learn. 15 I enjoyed drawing, coloring, and reading storybooks. 16 Sometimes we created our own games at home. 17 I loved reading even when I didn’t understand every word. 18 My childhood was full of fun, love, and laughter. 19 These experiences shaped my cheerful personality. 20 I learned the importance of family and simple joys in life.

Teenage Years

1 My teenage years were unique, especially during the pandemic. 2 I experienced my first crush in high school. 3 Because of online classes, we rarely saw each other in person. 4 Eventually, we lost connection, teaching me patience and acceptance. 5 I then focused more on my academics. 6 I chose the STEM strand in Senior High School. 7 Math and science became my favorite subjects. 8 I ranked within the top ten of my school. 9 Despite online learning challenges, Senior High was enjoyable. 10 We completed online projects and activities. 11 I bonded with classmates through games and study sessions. 12 I learned proper time management. 13 Teachers motivated us to push beyond our limits. 14 I developed new interests, including science experiments. 15 I stayed up late preparing for exams. 16 Weekends were spent resting with my family. 17 I became more independent and responsible. 18 I began thinking seriously about my future career. 19 Discipline and focus became important lessons. 20 These years prepared me for college life.

College Years

1 I started college at the University of the East – Manila at age seventeen. 2 I felt younger than most of my classmates. 3 My birthday is April 1, while classes started in August. 4 College life was very different from Senior High School. 5 Subjects became more focused on my chosen course, BSIT. 6 I initially wanted to become a doctor like my mother. 7 My father encouraged me to pursue Information Technology. 8 I agreed and eventually grew to love the course. 9 My first semester was tiring due to full-day schedules. 10 I made many friends and learned teamwork. 11 College taught me discipline and time management. 12 Most projects were computer-related, which I enjoyed. 13 I discovered new software and technical tools. 14 I joined online communities to improve my skills. 15 Balancing school and social life was challenging. 16 I learned to take responsibility for my choices. 17 Programming and networking became my favorite subjects. 18 Some days were difficult but meaningful. 19 College taught me patience and perseverance. 20 Overall, college life continues to shape who I am today.
